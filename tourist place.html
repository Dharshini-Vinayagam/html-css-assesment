1. Chennai

Chennai, India is known for many things, including its beaches, culture, and history: Marina Beach: One of the longest beaches in the world, stretching 12 km along the Bay of Bengal. It's a popular spot for watching the sunrise and sunset, and has food stalls and souvenir shops. Culture: Chennai is known as the cultural capital of South India, and is a major center for art, music, and film. It's also an important center for Bharatanatyam classical dance. History: Chennai has a rich history, and is the gateway to the rest of South India. The city was originally known as Madras Patnam, and was the capital of the Madras Presidency, which became the state of Tamil Nadu after India gained independence.

2. Madurai

Madurai is a city in Tamil Nadu, India, known for its rich cultural heritage, festivals, and temples: Meenakshi Amman Temple: The city's most famous landmark, this temple complex is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareshwar. It's one of the largest and oldest temple complexes in India, and is known for its intricate carvings and sculptures. The temple complex includes the Ayiramkaal Mandapam, or Hall of Thousand Pillars, and musical pillars that produce different notes when tapped. Festivals: Madurai is known as a "City of Festivals" and hosts many festivals throughout the year, including the Chithirai Festival, which honors Meenakshi and Lord Vishnu. History: Madurai was the capital of the Pandyan Kingdom from the 4th to 11th century CE, and has been conquered by many rulers, including the Cholas, Vijayanagars, Muslims, Marathas, and British. Other attractions: Other attractions in Madurai include Alagar Koil, Thiruparangkundram, Palarmuthir solai, Thirumalai naicker Mahal, and Teppakulam. Jasmine flowers: Madurai is famous for jasmine flowers. Jigardhanda: A typical Muslim cool drink that's famous in Madurai.

3. Thanjavur

Thanjavur, a city in Tamil Nadu, is known for many things, including its temples, art, and culture: Temples Thanjavur is home to the Great Living Chola temples, a UNESCO World Heritage site. The main attraction is the Brihadeeswara temple, a 212 ft (64.8 meter) tall Shiva temple that houses one of the largest Shiva Lingas in the country. The temple also has a 25-ton Nandi statue carved from a single stone. Thanjavur paintings Thanjavur paintings are known for their use of gold foil, lace, and semiprecious stones. The style continues to inspire contemporary artists, who have combined it with other styles to create mixed media arts. Carnatic music Thanjavur is the birthplace of Sri Thyagaraja, a legendary musician-composer of Carnatic music. Dancing dolls Thanjavur is famous for its "Thalaiyatti bommai" or "Dancing dolls", which are made from clay, wood, or plastic. Silk and cotton saris Thanjavur is a shopper's paradise and is the best place to buy handloom silk and cotton saris. Bell metal craft Thanjavur is known for its bell metal craft, including bronze images, bowls, napkins, and powder boxes. Pith works Thanjavur is a major manufacturer of pith works, which are models of Hindu idols, mosques, garlands, and other bird figurines.

4. Rameswaram

Renowned for being a pilgrimage destination, Rameswaram is home to the famous Ramanathaswamy Temple, known for its elaborate corridors and splendidly sculpted pillars. Though lying separated from the mainland of India in the Pamban Island, Rameswaram is connected to the mainland by the magnificent Pamban Bridge.

5. Kanniyakumari

Kanyakumari, a town in Tamil Nadu, India, is famous for many things, including: Beaches: Kanyakumari is a coastal town with great beaches. Monuments: Kanyakumari has many monuments that showcase its rich cultural heritage. Sunrise and sunset: Kanyakumari is the only place in India where you can watch the sunset and moonrise at the same time on a full moon. Vivekananda Rock Memorial and Thiruvalluvar Statue: These two popular attractions are located side by side in the sea. Pilgrimage center: Kanyakumari is a major pilgrimage center for Hindus. Shiva temple: Kanyakumari is known for its Shiva temple. Memorial to Mahatma Gandhi: Kanyakumari has a memorial to Mahatma Gandhi. Location: Kanyakumari is the southernmost town in mainland India, and is sometimes called "The Land's End". Confluence of seas: Kanyakumari is the meeting point of the Arabian Sea, Bay of Bengal, and Indian Ocean. Name: The town is named after the goddess Kanya Kumari, the virgin goddess, who legend says killed a demon on the site.

Yelagiri Hills is one of the hill stations of Tamil Nadu. Yelagiri hill top can be reached by a winding ghat road, that has 14 hairpin bends. The seventh hairpin bend is significant, since it offers the view of the slop of the mountain and the green forest covering as a carpet of the hill. Punganur Lake which is an artificial lake made in an are of 56.706 square metres. Boating on the waters of this lake can be an enchanting experience. 8. Kodaikanal

Kodaikanal is one of the very popular holiday destination hill resorts in South India. This hill station stands 7200 feet above sea level and situated in upper palani hills of the westernghats near Madurai in Tamil Nadu. Kodaikanal is also popularly known as the princes of Hill Stations. The cool and misty weather, the scenic beauty of the rolling hills and the wooded forest of Kodaikanal and its surroundings will mesmerize any visitor throughout the year. Walk through the wooded forests, row in the lake, bath in one of the splashing waterfalls, go for horse riding and cycling around the lake or simply admire the views. Kodaikanal Observatory The Kodaikanal Observatory of the Indian Institute of Astrophysics is located in the beautiful Palani range of hills in Southern India. It was established in 1899 as a Solar Physics Observatory and all the activities of the Madras Observatory were shifted to Kodaikanal. 20cm Refractor A 20 cm refractor at the Observatory is used occasionally for cometary and occultation observations. It is also sometimes made available to visitors for night sky viewing. Astronomy Museum The Observatory has arranged a popular Astronomy museum on campus for the visitors. The displays are mainly pictorial, supported by a few models. A live solar image and the Fraunhofer spectrum are also presented. From April 1st to June 15th Kodaikanal Observatory is open to public between 10:00 to 12:00 hrs and 14:00 to 16:00 hrs. For the rest of the year the Observatory will be open to public only on every Friday between 10:00 to 12:00 hrs.

9. Ooty

Ooty, also known as Udhagamandalam, is a picturesque hill station nestled in the Nilgiri Hills of southern India. It is located in the state of Tamil Nadu and is a popular tourist destination due to its breathtaking natural beauty and pleasant climate. Why is Ooty called the "Queen of Hill Stations"? Ooty earned the title "Queen of Hill Stations" due to its captivating landscapes, lush green valleys, and rolling hills. The town is surrounded by the Nilgiri Mountains, which are part of the Western Ghats, and is home to numerous tea gardens, dense forests, and beautiful lakes. What are some interesting facts about Ooty? 1. Ooty is situated at an altitude of 2,240 meters (7,350 feet) above sea level, making it one of the highest hill stations in India. 2. The Nilgiri Mountain Railway, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, connects Ooty with the town of Mettupalayam. It is renowned for its scenic beauty and operates a charming toy train. 3. Ooty is famous for its tea production. The region's tea gardens produce some of the finest tea in India, known for their distinct flavor and aroma. 4. The Government Botanical Garden in Ooty is one of the oldest and most well-maintained botanical gardens in India. It houses a vast collection of exotic plants, flowers, and trees. 5. Ooty Lake is a popular tourist attraction where visitors can enjoy boating and admire the serene surroundings. It was artificially constructed in the early 19th century.

10. Coimbatore

Coimbatore, Tamil Nadu is known for its many attractions, including religious sites, theme parks, and museums: Adiyogi Shiva Statue A 112-foot tall statue of Shiva, made from 500 tons of steel, that's located in the foothills of the Velliangiri Mountains. It's considered the world's largest bust sculpture and is listed in the Guinness World Records. Black Thunder Theme Park A family-friendly theme park with water games, a wave pool, rides, and more. Gass Forest Museum Established in 1902, this museum features embalmed specimens of rare species, as well as model houses, cultivation methods, and more. Insect Museum Part of the Agricultural University, this museum is home to collections dating back to 1900. G.D. Naidu Museum This museum features a collection of scientific devices and gadgets, as well as inventions by Gopalswamy Doraiswamy Naidu, who's known as the Edison of India. Regional Science Center This science park has a textile gallery, a Fun Science Gallery, a 3D theater, a giant globe, and a portable mini planetarium.
